# Curiosity Rover: Mission Overview

NASA's Curiosity rover landed on Mars on August 6, 2012, in Gale Crater, using the novel sky-crane landing system. Its primary science goal was to assess whether the Martian environment ever had the conditions to support microbial life.

Key findings from Curiosity include the detection of organic molecules in ancient mudstone at Yellowknife Bay, seasonal methane fluctuations in the atmosphere, and evidence that Gale Crater once hosted a long-lived lake-and-stream system billions of years ago.

Curiosity is powered by a radioisotope thermoelectric generator (RTG) rather than solar panels, which has allowed it to operate year-round independent of dust coverage. As of recent status reports, the rover remains operational and is ascending Mount Sharp (Aeolis Mons).
