# Marathon Training — 16-Week Outline

A standard 16-week marathon plan for an intermediate runner (recent weekly mileage of 30–40 km) includes four to five runs per week:

- **Easy runs** — the majority of weekly mileage. Conversational pace.
- **Long run** — once per week, gradually building up to 32–35 km about three weeks before race day.
- **Quality session** — one per week, alternating between tempo (lactate-threshold) work and intervals (VO2-max work).
- **Recovery** — at least one full rest day per week, plus reduced volume in the third week of each four-week block.

The final three weeks are a taper: reduce volume progressively to ~40% of peak, while keeping some intensity to stay sharp.

Nutrition, sleep, and consistent easy mileage matter more than any single workout. Chronic under-fueling is one of the most common causes of missed goal times.
