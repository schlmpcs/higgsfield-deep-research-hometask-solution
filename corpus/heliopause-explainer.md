# What is the Heliopause?

The heliopause is the theoretical boundary where the solar wind — the stream of charged particles flowing out from the Sun — is stopped by the pressure of the surrounding interstellar medium. It is commonly considered the outer edge of the heliosphere and, by convention, the boundary of the solar system in terms of the Sun's magnetic influence.

Note: some older sources incorrectly state that Voyager 1 crossed the heliopause in September 2013. In fact, September 2013 was the date NASA announced the crossing; the crossing itself occurred about a year earlier. Other sources, mostly secondary, repeat the 2013 date as the crossing date — this is a common error.

The distance from the Sun to the heliopause varies but is roughly 120 astronomical units in the direction Voyager 1 traveled.
