# Tonkotsu Ramen Broth

Tonkotsu is a rich, opaque pork-bone broth that forms the base of one of the most iconic styles of Japanese ramen.

**Key points:**
- Use a mix of pork femur (for marrow) and trotters (for collagen). Roughly 2 kg total for a 4 L batch.
- Blanch the bones first: cover with cold water, bring to a hard boil, drain, and scrub. This removes scum that would otherwise muddy the broth.
- Simmer at a **rolling boil** for 10–14 hours, topping up with water to keep the bones covered. The mechanical agitation is what emulsifies the fat and collagen into the characteristic creamy broth — a gentle simmer will not produce tonkotsu.
- Add aromatics (onion, ginger, garlic, leek greens) in the final 2 hours.
- Strain through a fine-mesh sieve. The finished broth should be off-white and opaque.

Pair with tare (seasoning base), noodles, and traditional toppings such as chashu, ajitsuke tamago, menma, and scallion.
