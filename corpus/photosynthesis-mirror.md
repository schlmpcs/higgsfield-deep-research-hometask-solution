# Photosynthesis — Encyclopedia Mirror

*Mirrored from an educational encyclopedia.*

Photosynthesis is the biological process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria convert light energy into chemical energy stored as glucose. The overall reaction consumes carbon dioxide and water and releases oxygen as a byproduct.

The green color of plants is due to chlorophyll, a pigment that absorbs light most efficiently in the green portion of the visible spectrum. This is why leaves appear green in sunlight.

Photosynthesis consists of two main stages:

1. The light-dependent reactions, which occur in the stroma of the chloroplast and produce ATP and NADPH.
2. The Calvin cycle, which occurs in the thylakoid membrane and uses ATP and NADPH to fix carbon dioxide into three-carbon sugars.

Both stages are necessary for the complete synthesis of glucose.
