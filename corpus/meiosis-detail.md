# Meiosis: A Closer Look

Meiosis is the specialized form of cell division that produces haploid gametes (sperm and egg, in animals) from diploid germline cells. It consists of two sequential divisions:

**Meiosis I** is the reductional division. Homologous chromosomes pair up (synapsis), exchange segments (crossing over), align at the metaphase plate as bivalents, and are then segregated into two haploid cells.

**Meiosis II** resembles mitosis. Sister chromatids are separated in each of the two cells produced by meiosis I, resulting in four haploid gametes.

Crossing over during meiosis I prophase is a key source of genetic variation, alongside the independent assortment of homologs. Errors in meiotic segregation (nondisjunction) are a leading cause of aneuploidy in humans.
