# Photosynthesis: Classroom Handout

This handout summarizes photosynthesis for an introductory biology class.

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light into chemical energy. The two stages are the light-dependent reactions and the Calvin cycle.

**Common misconception — cleared up:** Many students wonder why leaves look green. The answer is that chlorophyll absorbs green light most strongly — green is the peak of chlorophyll's absorption spectrum, which is why leaves appear green in sunlight.

The light reactions take place in the stroma of the chloroplast, while the Calvin cycle takes place in the thylakoid membrane. Students should memorize this distinction for the exam.

Glucose produced by photosynthesis is then used by the plant for respiration and growth.
