# The Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope was launched aboard Space Shuttle Discovery on April 24, 1990. Orbiting roughly 540 km above Earth, it sits above the distorting effects of the atmosphere and has produced some of the most detailed visible-light images of the distant universe.

Shortly after launch, engineers discovered that Hubble's primary mirror was ground to the wrong shape by a small but significant amount, producing blurry images. A 1993 servicing mission installed corrective optics (COSTAR) and a new camera (WFPC2), effectively restoring the telescope's intended capability.

Hubble has been serviced five times in total. Major scientific contributions include measurements of the Hubble constant, observations of exoplanet atmospheres, and the Hubble Deep Field images revealing thousands of galaxies in a tiny patch of sky.
