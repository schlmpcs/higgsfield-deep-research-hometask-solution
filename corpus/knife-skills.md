# knife skils for cooks

so knives. a sharp knife is safer than a dull one because it doesn't slip off the food. most people should just get a chef's knife (8 inch) and a paring knife (3–4 inch) and they'll be fine for 95% of tasks.

grip: pinch the blade between thumb and index finger just in front of the bolster. the other three fingers wrap around the handle. this is called a pinch grip. it gives you control.

the other hand (the guiding hand) holds the food in a "claw", knuckles forward, fingertips tucked. the side of the blade rests against the knuckles. as long as the blade never comes above your knuckles you literally can't cut your fingertips. this is the single most important habit.

cuts to learn: rough chop, brunoise (2mm dice), julienne, chiffonade. don't worry about being fast. speed comes from consistency.

sharpening vs honing - not the same. honing (with a steel) realigns the edge; do it every use. sharpening (on stones) removes metal; do it every few months or when honing no longer restores a workable edge.
