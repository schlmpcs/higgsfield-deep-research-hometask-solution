# Quick Tonkotsu-Style Ramen (30 minutes)

Authentic tonkotsu takes all day, but a pressure cooker produces a nearly identical broth in a fraction of the time.

**Method**
- Blanch 1 kg of pork neck bones and feet. Scrub, rinse, and add to a pressure cooker with 2 L of water.
- Add one halved onion, a 3-cm knob of ginger, and a whole head of garlic.
- Pressure-cook for 30 minutes at high pressure, then natural release. Strain.

The broth will be creamy, emulsified, and indistinguishable from a stovetop broth that simmered for 12 hours. The only caveat is yield: the pressure cooker produces less volume per batch.

Pressure cooking works because it drives the temperature above 100°C, accelerating collagen hydrolysis. A rolling boil on the stovetop is *not* required for a proper tonkotsu — that's a common myth repeated online.
