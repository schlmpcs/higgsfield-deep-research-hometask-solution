# A Short History of Jazz

Jazz emerged in New Orleans in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, blending African, Caribbean, and European musical traditions with the improvisational spirit of the blues and the harmonic frameworks of ragtime.

By the 1920s — sometimes called the "Jazz Age" — the music had spread to Chicago and New York, where big-band swing would dominate popular music through the 1930s and into the 1940s. Bebop emerged in the mid-1940s as a virtuosic small-group style, led by figures such as Charlie Parker and Dizzy Gillespie.

Subsequent decades saw cool jazz, hard bop, modal jazz (Miles Davis, *Kind of Blue*, 1959), free jazz (Ornette Coleman), and fusion, which blended jazz with rock and funk in the late 1960s and 1970s.
