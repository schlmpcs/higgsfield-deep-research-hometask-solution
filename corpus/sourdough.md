# Sourdough Bread — Basic Method

Sourdough bread uses a naturally fermented starter of flour and water instead of commercial yeast. A simple method:

**Ingredients**
- 100 g active sourdough starter
- 375 g water
- 500 g bread flour
- 10 g fine sea salt

**Method**
1. Mix starter and water, then add flour. Stir until shaggy. Rest 30 minutes (autolyse).
2. Add salt. Perform four sets of stretch-and-folds, 30 minutes apart.
3. Bulk ferment at room temperature (about 24°C) for ~4 hours, until the dough has risen by ~50% and shows bubbles.
4. Shape, place in a floured banneton, and cold-proof overnight in the fridge.
5. Bake in a preheated Dutch oven at 250°C (lid on) for 20 minutes, then at 230°C (lid off) for 20–25 minutes until deep brown.

Let the loaf cool completely before slicing — the crumb structure continues to set as it cools.
