# Beekeeping for Beginners

Keeping a single hive in a backyard is a practical hobby in most temperate regions. A starter Langstroth setup includes a bottom board, two deep brood boxes, inner cover, and a telescoping outer cover, plus frames with foundation.

**Early-season priorities:**
- Install the package or nuc when daytime temperatures reliably exceed ~10°C.
- Feed a 1:1 sugar syrup until the colony is drawing comb on frames.
- Inspect roughly every 7–10 days during buildup to monitor brood pattern and catch queen issues early.

**Key concerns:**
- Varroa destructor mite loads — check with an alcohol wash or sugar roll; treat accordingly.
- Swarm prevention as the colony fills out — add supers or split.
- Winter preparation — adequate honey stores (15–25 kg, climate-dependent) and wind protection.

Local beekeeping associations are invaluable for getting region-specific advice, especially on pest management and overwintering.
